The Bust of Nefertiti — A 3D Journey Through Time

The Discovery

On December 6, 1912, in the ancient city of Amarna, German archaeologist Ludwig Borchardt unearthed a painted limestone bust from the workshop of the royal sculptor Thutmose. It had been buried for over 3,300 years.

The Queen

Nefertiti — "the beautiful one has come" — was the Great Royal Wife of Pharaoh Akhenaten. Together they led a religious revolution, overthrowing Egypt's ancient gods to worship the sun disk Aten, and built an entire new capital from the desert.

The Unfinished Eye

This bust was a sculptor's master model, kept in Thutmose's workshop for apprentices to study. One eye remains unfinished — whether by accident, as a teaching tool, or for reasons lost to time, we may never know. Today she resides in the Neues Museum, Berlin, having survived wars, looting, and the centuries.
